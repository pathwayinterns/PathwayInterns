PIVOT: International Youth-Led Think Tank

About: PIVOT is an international and remote youth-led think tank that empowers young people ages 13–19 to research, write, and advocate for policy solutions that address today’s most pressing issues. Our mission is to ensure youth voices are heard at every level of government—local, national, and global. Through collaborative research cohorts, mentorship from policy professionals, and real-world advocacy opportunities, PIVOT fellows are given the tools to turn their ideas into impact. The fellowship is indefinite, offers opportunities to publish original research, and allows fellows to later step into leadership roles. Our team currently includes over 50 youth across the world.

Project Light Shade

About: ProjectLightShade is a youth-led initiative dedicated to advocating for sun safety and skincare equity, particularly for Black and brown communities. Despite the misconception that melanin offers complete protection from the sun, our communities remain vulnerable to sun damage, skin cancer, and hyperpigmentation. ProjectLightShade seeks to raise awareness, educate, and promote healthier skincare practices while addressing the disparities in the skincare industry. Our mission is to empower individuals to take control of their skin health and to fight for greater representation and inclusivity in sun safety and skincare.

California Association of Student Councils (CASC) Summer Leadership Conference

About: The California Association of Student Councils is an organization founded by the CA Department of Education in 1947. CASC’s student leaders work to implement real legislative change in the state, and the Summer Leadership Conference equips attendees with the skills that they need to make change in their communities. The Summer Leadership Conference will be held from July 28th to 31st at UC Santa Barbara. Attendees & CASC cabinet have gone on to T20 Universities including Harvard, UPenn, Columbia, and Cornell, and this conference helps students gain experience to become more involved with CASC as a whole. Final registration ends on July 12th.

Stories Under The Skin

About: Stories Under The Skin is a youth-led initiative exploring the intersection of physical and mental health through interviews with medical professionals, psychological insights, and student-written reflections. The project aims to amplify the voices of those navigating chronic illness and mental health challenges. Roles currently available include Writing Fellow, Featured Contributor, Youth Ambassador, and Outreach Partner. Team recruitment is also open—interested individuals are encouraged to connect via social media or email.

Wellness Without Borders

About: Wellness Without Borders is an organization started by high school students to give medical supplies and funding to individuals in underserved communities. With over 7,000 tons of clean, usable medical supplies in the U.S., worth more than $20 billion, we aim to bridge the gap between excess and need. By redirecting these resource to clinics and hospitals in dire need, WWB ensures that no medical resource goes to waste while countless individuals lack essential care. We believe that every community, regardless of their economic situation, deserves access to the medical supplies that can save lives!
